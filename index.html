Chess puzzle

This is a single-player puzzle derived from the rules of chess, played on a 4x4 board. Starting with white, play alternates between moving a white and then a black piece. Every move must capture a piece of the opposing colour. Pieces move just like in chess, but there is no check. To successfully solve the puzzle, capture every piece leaving behind only the white king.

The initial configuration is a random permutation of the 16 pieces of a normal chess set, with pawns excluded.

Open questions include: What is the total number of unique starting positions? Is every starting position solvable? If not, what proportion are solvable? Is there a quick way to prove whether a position is solvable without actually solving it? Is there a deterministic solving algorithm that doesn't need any brute force search?

By James Stanley.
